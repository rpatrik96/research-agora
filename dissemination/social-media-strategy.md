# Social Media Strategy — Patrik Reizinger

General visibility strategy for an ML researcher. Research Agora is one project, not the whole identity.
The work comes first. Social media amplifies the work — it does not replace it.

## Tier 1 — The 80/20 (~2 hrs/month)

This is the minimum viable strategy. Do only this if research time is scarce.

### 1. Keep the newsletter alive (90 min/month)

The Path to PhD on Substack. This is the only audience you own — no algorithm can cut it.

- **Frequency:** Monthly (12 issues/year). Biweekly if you have bandwidth.
- **Each issue:** 1 main piece (600-1000 words) + "what I read" section (3-5 links with 1-sentence comments)
- **Content:** 60% PhD reflections, 30% ML research insights, 10% tools

### 2. Tweet when you have something real (~15 min/post, as-needed)

Post a thread when you ship something: paper, tool release, interesting insight. Do not post to "maintain presence."

**Rules:**
- Link goes in first reply, never in tweet body (30-50% reach penalty)
- End every thread with newsletter CTA in the reply
- Pin your best explainer thread, not a self-promo tweet

### 3. Create one canonical resource (one-time, 20-40 hrs)

This is 80% of all social media growth distilled into one action.

Every researcher who built a large following did it through an **asset**, not tweets:
- Saravia: Prompt Engineering Guide → 50K GitHub stars
- Chip Huyen: ML Interviews book (free) → viral spread
- Lilian Weng: Survey blog posts → 5-year compounding
- Raschka: Newsletter → 174K subscribers

**Your move:** One definitive survey/explainer/guide at the intersection of causal ML + your tooling expertise, on an emerging topic 3-6 months before saturation. GitHub repo or blog post. This single artifact will do more than 12 months of tweeting.

## Tier 2 — Steady State (~1 hr/week)

Add this when you have a sustainable rhythm and want to grow intentionally.

### Weekly Routine

| Day | What | Time |
|-----|------|------|
| Sun | Batch-write 2-3 tweets + 1 LinkedIn post for the week | 30 min |
| When published | Cross-post newsletter teaser to Twitter + LinkedIn | 10 min |
| Spare moments | 2-3 substantive replies to ML researchers (5k-50k followers) | 15 min |
| **Total** | | **~55 min/week** |

### Newsletter-to-Social Flywheel

Each newsletter issue generates social content for free:
1. Day 0: Hook tweet (no link) → newsletter link in first reply
2. Day 1-2: Extract most counterintuitive insight as standalone tweet
3. Optional: Repurpose as LinkedIn text post

### LinkedIn Minimal Strategy

- Post 1-2x/week when active, skip weeks when you're heads-down
- NO external links in post body (60% reach penalty) — links go in first comment
- Carousel/PDF posts get 3x reach of text — worth the extra 15 min to make one when announcing something

### Content Mix (when you do post)

- 40% paper commentary / explainers (your domain, others' papers)
- 25% curation ("what I read this month")
- 20% tools / projects (Research Agora and others)
- 15% PhD process / behind-the-research

### The 30% Rule
No more than 30% of content about any single project. The brand is the person, not the project.

### Additional Growth Tactics

- 5 substantive replies/day to researchers (asymmetric leverage — one good reply on a viral thread drives 50-200 followers)
- Comment on 3-5 LinkedIn posts BEFORE publishing your own (primes algorithm)
- Respond to LinkedIn comments within 15 minutes (90% algorithmic boost)
- Seek 2-3 Substack cross-recommendations with peer newsletters
- One LinkedIn poll/month ("What's the hardest open problem in X?")
- Conference live-tweeting during NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR

## Brand Identity

### Positioning

**Internal compass:** "He builds research tools grounded in the same rigor he brings to causal ML theory."

**External shorthand:** "He does causal ML theory and builds the tools researchers actually need."

### Four Pillars

| Pillar | What it means | Content signal |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Rigorous Foundations** | Causal representation learning, identifiability, mathematical ML | Paper threads, theory explainers |
| **Research as Engineering** | Building research infrastructure is a research contribution | Tool announcements, workflow posts |
| **Knowledge Architecture** | Structuring how knowledge moves between researchers | Newsletter, tutorials, teaching |
| **The Practitioner-Theorist** | Bridge between deep theory and applied ML | Cross-domain commentary |

### Identity Layers

```
Layer 3 (broadest): ML researcher who makes complex things understandable
Layer 2 (medium):   ML researcher who builds tools and explains ideas
Layer 1 (specific): Causal rep learning, identifiability theory
```

Operate at Layer 2-3 in public. Layer 1 is your credibility floor.

### Tone of Voice

| Dimension | Target | Avoid |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Formality | Conference talk | LinkedIn corporate or shitposting |
| Confidence | State claims directly | Hedging everything; overstating |
| Accessibility | Define terms once, use freely | Dumbing down; gatekeeping |
| Opinion | Take positions, show reasoning | Substance-free hot takes |
| Humor | Dry, observational, rare | Forced memes |

## Content Repurposing (When You Do Post)

```
ROOT IDEA (Obsidian note / research notebook)
    ├── Twitter thread (immediate, informal)
    │     └── LinkedIn post (polished, no link in body)
    │           └── Newsletter section (expanded, personal)
    └── GitHub release → Twitter → LinkedIn carousel → Newsletter
```

### Paper Announcement Template

| Platform | Format | Key rule |
|----------|--------|----------|
| Twitter | 6-7 tweet thread: problem → method → figure → implications | Hook = punchline, not setup. ArXiv link in last tweet |
| LinkedIn | Text + figure image → "link in comments" | No link in body |
| Newsletter | "Why I worked on this" + open questions | Personal story angle |
| GitHub | Release code + paper link in README | BibTeX block |

### Tool Release Template

| Platform | Format | Key rule |
|----------|--------|----------|
| GitHub | README first (forces clarity) | What → Why → Install → Example |
| Twitter | 4-6 tweets: problem → demo → install | Link in reply only |
| LinkedIn | 3 paragraphs + screenshot → CTA | Link in comment |
| Newsletter | Motivation + failed approaches + what worked | Personal story |

## Anti-Patterns

1. **Don't become "the [project] guy."** Projects are expressions of the brand, not the brand.
2. **Don't split into two personas.** Theory and tooling should feel like the same intellectual project.
3. **Don't do generic PhD advice.** Write about how *you* think, not repackaged tips.
4. **Don't chase engagement bait.** Every post: "this person sees something I don't."
5. **Don't over-index on affiliation.** Tübingen/ELLIS/MPI-IS are signals, not identity.
6. **Don't apologize for being theoretical.** The theory is the foundation.
7. **Don't comment on every AI trend.** Silence on trending topics signals seriousness.
8. **Don't neglect visuals.** Consistent visual identity compounds recognition.

## Evolution Path

### The Invariant Core

Regardless of career path:
1. **Think rigorously.** Theorems, tools, or companies — first principles.
2. **Build things.** Ship, don't just write about it.
3. **Share what you learn.** Papers, tools, newsletters, talks.

If all three are present, the brand is intact.

### Post-PhD Transitions

| Path | What strengthens | What evolves | Update needed |
|------|-----------------|--------------|---------------|
| Industry lab | Foundations (more compute) | Engineering → "at scale" | "PhD" → role title |
| Startup | Practitioner-Theorist | Knowledge Architecture → marketing | "Researcher" → "Founder" |
| PI track | All pillars | Engineering → "the lab that builds tools" | "PhD" → "Professor" |

## Case Studies (TL;DR)

| Researcher | Key lesson |
|-----------|------------|
| **Raschka** | Consistent deep writing > frequent shallow posting. Newsletter > Twitter |
| **Chip Huyen** | High utility density per piece. One book > years of tweets |
| **Saravia** | Time a canonical resource to an emerging topic → explosive compounding |
| **Lilian Weng** | 4-8 landmark posts/year beats daily posting |
| **Kilcher** | YouTube is underexplored for European ML researchers |

**Common pattern:** They built followings by creating *assets* and using social media as the announcement layer.

## Summary: Pick Your Tier

| Tier | Time | What you do | Expected outcome |
|------|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **0** | 45 min once | Fix bios, headline, About, Featured | Stop losing visitors who check your profile |
| **1** | ~2 hrs/month | Newsletter + tweet when you ship + one canonical resource | Slow organic growth, owned audience via Substack |
| **2** | ~1 hr/week | Tier 1 + weekly batch posting + newsletter flywheel | Steady growth, name recognition in your niche |
| **3** | ~3-4 hrs/week | Full calendar + engagement tactics + analytics | Active growth mode toward 10K Twitter, 3K LinkedIn |

**Recommendation:** Do Tier 0 today. Stay at Tier 1 by default. Move to Tier 2 when you have a rhythm. Use Tier 3 only during launch windows or when you actively want to grow.

The work is the brand. Everything else is amplification.
